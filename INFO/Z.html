Nissan Z

The Nissan Z, also known as the Nissan 400Z in its latest generation, is the spiritual successor to Nissan’s legendary Z-car line that started with the 240Z in the 1970s. The newest Z combines classic sports car styling with modern performance, technology, and comfort, appealing to enthusiasts who love Japanese performance cars with heritage.

The 2023+ Nissan Z features a 3.0 L twin-turbo V6 engine, producing strong power and torque while maintaining a rear-wheel-drive layout for the true sports car experience. With a six-speed manual or nine-speed automatic transmission, the Z offers precise handling, sharp acceleration, and a driver-focused cockpit that honors its Z lineage.

Today, the Nissan Z continues the legacy of blending performance, heritage, and affordability in a sports car package. It represents Nissan’s commitment to producing a fun-to-drive sports car that can compete with other modern Japanese and international rivals.

Engine
  • 3.0 L VR30DDTT V6 Twin-Turbo
  • Power: 400 hp
  • Torque: 475 Nm
  • Redline: 7,000 rpm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 6-Speed Manual Transmission
  • 9-Speed Automatic Transmission
  • Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD)
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: ~4.5 s
  • Top Speed: ~250 km/h
  • Quarter Mile: ~12 s
Chassis & Suspension
  • Front: Independent double-wishbone
  • Rear: Multi-link suspension
  • Sport-tuned adaptive dampers
Brakes
  • Ventilated disc brakes
  • ABS + EBD + Stability control
  • Optional Brembo brakes (Nismo)
Dimensions
  • Length: ~4,380 mm
  • Width: ~1,865 mm
  • Height: ~1,310 mm
  • Wheelbase: ~2,550 mm
  • Weight: ~1,540 kg
Interior & Practicality
  • 2-seater layout
  • Analog + digital driver cockpit
  • Premium sport seats & materials
  • Minimal cargo space (typical sports car)
