Nissan Skyline R34

The Nissan Skyline R34, produced between 1999 and 2002, is one of the most iconic Japanese performance cars of all time. As part of the legendary Skyline GT-R lineage, the R34 combined cutting-edge technology, advanced aerodynamics, and the RB26DETT twin-turbo inline-six engine to create a car that was both track-capable and street-legendary. Its distinctive design, aggressive stance, and advanced features made it a symbol of Japanese automotive engineering excellence.

The R34 was equipped with the ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system and an active limited-slip rear differential (Super-HICAS), giving it exceptional handling and cornering capabilities. It gained massive popularity worldwide due to its appearance in racing games, movies like the Fast & Furious series, and the car’s tunability for high horsepower builds. Despite being a car from the late 90s, its digital dashboard, multifunction display, and precise performance controls were ahead of its time.

Today, the Skyline R34 is a collector’s dream, revered by enthusiasts for its balance of power, handling, and classic JDM styling. It remains a benchmark for Japanese performance cars, representing the golden era of Nissan’s Skyline GT-R development.

Engine
  • RB26DETT 2.6 L Inline-6 Twin-Turbo
  • Power: 280 PS (officially, ~276 hp) at 6,800 rpm
  • Torque: 353 Nm at 4,400 rpm
  • Redline: 8,000 rpm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 6-Speed Getrag Manual Transmission
  • ATTESA E-TS All-Wheel Drive System
  • Active Limited-Slip Rear Differential (Super-HICAS)
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: ~5.4 seconds
  • Top Speed: ~250 km/h (electronically limited)
  • Quarter Mile: ~13.0 seconds
Chassis & Suspension
  • Front: Independent double wishbone
  • Rear: Multi-link independent
  • MacPherson struts for steering precision
Brakes
  • Ventilated disc brakes front and rear
  • ABS with traction control
Dimensions
  • Length: ~4,565 mm
  • Width: ~1,735 mm
  • Height: ~1,335 mm
  • Wheelbase: ~2,640 mm
  • Weight: ~1,540 kg
Interior & Practicality
  • 2+2 seating layout
  • Driver-oriented cockpit with multifunction display
  • Analog and digital combination dashboard
  • Basic cargo space suitable for a performance car
