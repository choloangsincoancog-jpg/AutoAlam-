Lamborghini Aventador

The Lamborghini Aventador is a flagship V12 supercar introduced in 2011, known for its aggressive design, extreme performance, and iconic naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 engine. It replaced the Murciélago as Lamborghini’s top model.

With up to 770 horsepower in the SVJ variant, a 7-speed ISR gearbox, all-wheel drive, and advanced aerodynamics, the Aventador delivers explosive acceleration, precise handling, and a dramatic presence on the road.

Over the years, variants like the Aventador S, SV, and SVJ have improved power, aerodynamics, and technology, making it a symbol of Lamborghini’s engineering and the last true naturally aspirated V12 supercar of its era.

Engine
  • 6.5 L Naturally Aspirated V12
  • Displacement: 6,498 cc
  • Power (LP700-4): 700 PS
  • Power (Aventador S): 740 PS
  • Power (SV): 750 PS
  • Power (SVJ): 770 PS
  • Torque: ~690 Nm
  • Redline: ~8,500 rpm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 7-Speed ISR single-clutch transmission
  • All-Wheel Drive (Haldex system)
  • Electronically controlled center differential
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h (LP700-4): 2.9 s
  • 0–100 km/h (SV/SVJ): 2.8 s
  • Top Speed: ~350 km/h
  • Quarter Mile: ~10–11 seconds
  • Drive Modes: Strada, Sport, Corsa (Ego Mode for newer models)
Chassis & Suspension
  • Carbon-fiber monocoque chassis
  • Aluminum front and rear subframes
  • Push-rod suspension system
  • Magnetorheological dampers (S, SVJ)
  • Four-wheel steering (Aventador S onward)
Brakes
  • Carbon-ceramic brakes
  • Ventilated discs
  • 6-piston front calipers
  • 4-piston rear calipers

Interior & Practicality
  • Two-seat cockpit
  • TFT digital instrument cluster
  • Front luggage compartment (~140 L)
  • Drive-by-wire throttle system
  • Optional luxury trims depending on variant
