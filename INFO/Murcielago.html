Lamborghini Murciélago

The Lamborghini Murciélago is one of the most iconic V12 supercars ever produced. Introduced in 2001, it marked the beginning of the modern Lamborghini era under Audi ownership. Designed by Luc Donckerwolke, the Murciélago combined sharp Italian aggression with German engineering precision.

The Murciélago is powered by a legendary 6.2-liter (later 6.5-liter) naturally aspirated V12 engine, producing up to 661 hp in the LP 670-4 SV variant. Power is sent through either a gated 6-speed manual or Lamborghini’s E-Gear automated manual transmission.

Known for its scissor doors, extreme aerodynamics, and incredible performance, the Murciélago became a symbol of the ultimate early-2000s supercar—continuing Lamborghini’s legacy of dramatic V12 machines.

Engine
  • 6.2 L Naturally Aspirated V12 (2001–2006)
  • 6.5 L Naturally Aspirated V12 (LP640 & SV)
  • 572–661 hp depending on variant
  • Signature Lamborghini V12 sound
Transmission
  • 6-speed gated manual
  • 6-speed E-Gear automated manual
  • All-wheel drive (AWD)
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: 3.2–3.8 seconds (varies by model)
  • Top Speed: 330–342 km/h
  • Weight: ~1,650 kg
Chassis
  • Carbon fiber & steel tubular frame
  • All-wheel drive system
  • Electronically controlled aerodynamics
Brakes
  • Brembo ventilated discs
  • Optional carbon-ceramic brakes (LP640+)
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,579 mm
  • Width: 2,045 mm
  • Height: 1,135 mm
  • Wheelbase: 2,665 mm
