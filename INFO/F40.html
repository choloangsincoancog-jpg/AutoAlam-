Ferrari F40

The Ferrari F40 is one of the most iconic supercars ever built, created to celebrate Ferrari’s 40th anniversary. Introduced in 1987, it was the last Ferrari personally approved by Enzo Ferrari himself. Built with a focus on raw performance, the F40 features a lightweight body made from carbon fiber, Kevlar, and aluminum, combined with a twin-turbocharged V8 engine that delivers thrilling speed.

The F40 was engineered with pure driving in mind—no traction control, no ABS, no power steering, and no luxury features. Every aspect of the car was designed for performance, from its aerodynamic bodywork to its minimalistic interior. It quickly became a legend for its brutal acceleration, razor-sharp handling, and signature pop-up headlights.

Today, the Ferrari F40 remains one of the most desirable classic supercars, praised for its motorsport DNA, raw power, and unmatched connection between driver and machine. It embodies Ferrari’s golden era and continues to stand as a symbol of uncompromised performance.

Engine
  • 2.9 L Twin-Turbocharged V8
  • Power: 471 hp
  • Torque: 577 Nm
  • Redline: ~7,700 rpm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 5-speed manual transmission
  • Rear-wheel drive (RWD)
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: ~4.1 seconds
  • Top Speed: 324 km/h
  • Power-to-weight: ~356 hp/ton
Chassis & Suspension
  • Lightweight carbon fiber/Kevlar chassis
  • Front: Double wishbone with coil springs
  • Rear: Double wishbone with coil springs
  • Adjustable suspension (later models)
Brakes
  • Ventilated disc brakes
  • No ABS
  • Brembo high-performance braking system
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,360 mm
  • Width: 1,970 mm
  • Height: 1,120 mm
  • Wheelbase: 2,450 mm
  • Weight: ~1,100 kg
Interior & Practicality
  • 2 seats
  • Stripped-down racing interior
  • Carbon-fiber bucket seats
  • No air conditioning (early models), no radio
