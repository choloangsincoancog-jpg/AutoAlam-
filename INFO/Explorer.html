Ford Explorer

The Ford Explorer is a mid-size SUV that has become one of Ford’s most popular family and utility vehicles since its introduction in 1990. Over multiple generations, it has evolved from a rugged off-road SUV into a modern, versatile crossover blending comfort, technology, and performance.

The latest Explorer offers multiple powertrains, including EcoBoost engines and a hybrid option, giving buyers flexibility in fuel economy and performance. Intelligent all-wheel drive, terrain management systems, and modern safety tech make it capable both on-road and off-road.

Today, the Explorer remains a benchmark in the mid-size SUV segment with advanced tech like a digital gauge cluster, SYNC 4 infotainment, and spacious three-row seating—perfect for families and long trips.

Engine Options
  • 2.3 L EcoBoost I4 Turbo — 300 hp, 420 Nm
  • 3.0 L EcoBoost V6 — 400 hp, 583 Nm
  • 3.3 L Hybrid V6 — 318 hp combined, 430 Nm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 10-Speed Automatic
  • Rear-Wheel Drive (standard)
  • Intelligent All-Wheel Drive (optional)
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: ~6.0–7.0 sec
  • Top Speed: ~200–230 km/h
  • Towing Capacity: 2,268–2,750 kg
Chassis & Suspension
  • Independent front & rear suspension
  • Adaptive damping system (higher trims)
  • Terrain Management System
Brakes
  • Four-wheel disc brakes
  • ABS & stability control
  • Optional advanced braking assist
Dimensions
  • Length: 5,050 mm
  • Width: 2,004 mm
  • Height: 1,778 mm
  • Wheelbase: 3,025 mm
  • Weight: 2,100–2,400 kg
Interior & Practicality
  • Three-row seating (up to 7 passengers)
  • Digital cluster + SYNC 4 infotainment
  • Premium leather seating (higher trims)
  • Cargo space: 515–2,274 liters
