Ford Focus

The Ford Focus is a compact car that has been a mainstay in Ford’s lineup since 1998, offering a combination of affordability, performance, and technology. Designed for both urban commuting and enjoyable driving, the Focus has evolved through multiple generations to include hatchback, sedan, and wagon body styles. Its reputation for sharp handling, fuel efficiency, and advanced safety features has made it one of the most popular compact cars worldwide.

The latest Ford Focus models come with a range of powertrains, including EcoBoost turbocharged engines, naturally aspirated engines, and hybrid options in some markets. High-performance variants like the Focus ST and RS feature turbocharged engines, sport-tuned suspension, and advanced braking systems, giving them near-hot hatch performance while still being practical for daily use.

Engine Options
  • 1.0 L EcoBoost Inline-3: 100–125 hp, 170–200 Nm torque
  • 1.5 L EcoBoost Inline-4: 150–182 hp, 240–260 Nm torque
  • 2.3 L EcoBoost I4 (ST / RS): 280–350 hp, 440–470 Nm torque
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 5/6-Speed Manual Transmission
  • 8-Speed Automatic Transmission (optional)
  • Front-Wheel Drive (standard)
  • All-Wheel Drive (optional in RS)
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: 5.7–10.5 seconds depending on engine
  • Top Speed: 180–250 km/h depending on variant
  • Fuel Economy: 5.0–7.5 L/100 km depending on engine
Chassis & Suspension
  • Independent front MacPherson strut suspension
  • Multi-link rear suspension
  • Optional sport-tuned suspension (ST / RS)
Brakes
  • Disc brakes front and rear
  • ABS, electronic stability control
  • Upgraded Brembo brakes on ST / RS
Dimensions
  • Length: ~4,378 mm
  • Width: ~1,825 mm
  • Height: ~1,452 mm
  • Wheelbase: ~2,700 mm
  • Weight: ~1,250–1,500 kg depending on variant
Interior & Practicality
  • 5-seat configuration
  • Infotainment system with SYNC 3 or 4
  • Available premium upholstery
  • Hatchback and sedan variants for flexible cargo space (~375–1,354 liters)
