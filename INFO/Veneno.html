Lamborghini Veneno

The Lamborghini Veneno is a hypercar built to celebrate Lamborghini’s 50th anniversary, introduced in 2013. Its name, “Veneno,” means “venom” in Spanish, and it perfectly reflects the car’s aggressive design, extreme aerodynamics, and razor-sharp performance.

Based on the Aventador platform, the Veneno pushes engineering to the extreme with a naturally aspirated 6.5-liter V12 producing 750 PS (740 hp), a 7-speed ISR single-clutch gearbox, and all-wheel drive. Only 3 coupés and 9 roadsters were produced, making it one of the rarest Lamborghinis ever.

With a 0–100 km/h time of 2.8 seconds and a top speed of ~355 km/h, the Veneno remains one of the most extreme, exclusive, and iconic hypercars ever built.

Engine
  • 6.5 L Naturally Aspirated V12
  • 750 PS (740 hp)
  • 690 Nm torque
  • Redline: ~8,400 rpm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 7-Speed ISR Single-Clutch Transmission
  • All-Wheel Drive System
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: 2.8 seconds
  • Top Speed: ~355 km/h
  • Quarter Mile: ~9.9 seconds
Chassis & Suspension
  • Carbon-fiber monocoque
  • Push-rod suspension system
  • Carbon-ceramic high-performance brakes
Dimensions
  • Length: ~4,965 mm
  • Width: ~2,070 mm
  • Height: ~1,150 mm
  • Wheelbase: ~2,700 mm
  • Weight: ~1,490 kg
Interior & Practicality
  • 2-seat cockpit
  • Race-focused minimalist interior
  • Premium leather & Alcantara
  • Extremely limited cargo space
