Pagani Huayra

The Pagani Huayra, named after the Andean god of winds, is a hypercar that combines Italian craftsmanship with extreme engineering. First unveiled in 2011, it is known for its breathtaking attention to detail, active aerodynamics, and a twin-turbo V12 developed exclusively by Mercedes-AMG.


The Huayra’s active aero flaps, lightweight carbo-titanium chassis, and advanced hydropneumatic suspension allow it to carve corners with unmatched precision while maintaining luxurious comfort inside the cabin.


Every Huayra is hand-built, highly customizable, and produced in extremely limited numbers — making it one of the most exclusive and artistic hypercars ever created.

Engine
  • 6.0 L AMG Twin-Turbocharged V12 (M158)
  • Power: 720–754 hp (varies by model)
  • Torque: 1,000–1,100 Nm
  • Custom-built exclusively for Pagani
  • Carbo-Titanium monocoque compatibility
Transmission
  • 7-speed sequential gearbox (Xtrac)
  • Single clutch for lighter weight
  • Rear-wheel drive
  • Electronically controlled differential
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: 3.0 seconds
  • 0–200 km/h: ~9.5 seconds
  • Top Speed: 383 km/h (238 mph)
  • Active aerodynamics with 4 independent flaps
Chassis & Suspension
  • Carbo-Titanium monocoque chassis
  • Double wishbone suspension
  • Hydropneumatic adaptive dampers
  • Active aero integration for stability
Brakes
  • Brembo carbon-ceramic brakes
  • Lightweight 4-wheel ventilated discs
  • ABS and advanced brake cooling channels
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,605 mm
  • Width: 2,036 mm
  • Height: 1,169 mm
  • Weight: 1,350 kg (depends on variant)
Interior & Features
  • Luxury hand-crafted leather and aluminum
  • Fully exposed gear mechanism
  • Aircraft-inspired switchgear
  • Highly customizable interior trims
