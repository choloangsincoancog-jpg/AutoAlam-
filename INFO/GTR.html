Nissan GT-R

The Nissan GT-R (R35), introduced in 2007, is the modern successor to the legendary Skyline GT-R, carrying forward the GT-R name as Nissan’s flagship performance car. Known as “Godzilla” in the automotive world, the GT-R combines extreme performance, advanced technology, and Japanese engineering precision to dominate both road and track. Unlike its Skyline predecessors, the R35 drops the Skyline name entirely, establishing itself as a standalone supercar.

The GT-R features a 3.8 L twin-turbocharged V6 engine (VR38DETT) paired with a dual-clutch 6-speed transmission and advanced ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system. Its lightweight construction, adaptive suspension, and active aerodynamics allow it to deliver supercar-level acceleration and cornering, all while retaining daily usability. Its sophisticated electronics, including launch control and torque vectoring, make it one of the most driver-focused and precise high-performance cars of the modern era.

Today, the Nissan GT-R remains a symbol of Japanese performance and engineering, blending tunability, raw power, and technological sophistication. It continues to challenge supercars from Europe and America, proving that Japanese engineering can compete at the highest level.

Engine
  • 3.8 L VR38DETT V6 Twin-Turbo
  • Power: 570–600 hp (standard), up to 720+ hp in Nismo version
  • Torque: 637–652 Nm
  • Redline: ~7,000 rpm
Transmission & Drivetrain
  • 6-Speed Dual-Clutch Transmission
  • ATTESA E-TS All-Wheel Drive
  • Active Limited Slip Differential
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: 2.9–3.2 seconds (depending on version)
  • Top Speed: ~315 km/h
  • Quarter Mile: ~10.8–11.0 seconds
Chassis & Suspension
  • Front double-wishbone suspension
  • Rear multi-link suspension with adaptive dampers
  • Advanced aerodynamics for high-speed stability
Brakes
  • Brembo brake system with ventilated discs
  • ABS and electronic brake-force distribution
  • Carbon-ceramic brakes optional in Nismo version
Dimensions
  • Length: ~4,710 mm
  • Width: ~1,895 mm
  • Height: ~1,370 mm
  • Wheelbase: ~2,780 mm
  • Weight: ~1,740–1,800 kg depending on trim
Interior & Practicality
  • 2+2 seating layout
  • Driver-focused cockpit with multifunction digital displays
  • Premium materials and sports seats
  • Minimal cargo space, designed for performance
