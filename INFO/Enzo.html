Ferrari Enzo

The Ferrari Enzo, named in honor of company founder Enzo Ferrari, is one of the most iconic and technologically advanced supercars of the early 2000s. Produced in a limited run of **399 units**, it represented Ferrari’s most extreme road-going machine at the time.


Built using Formula 1–derived technology, the Enzo features a carbon-fiber monocoque chassis, advanced aerodynamics, ceramic brakes, and one of the most powerful naturally aspirated engines Ferrari has ever created.


With its legendary V12, rapid-shifting F1 gearbox, and lightweight carbon construction, the Ferrari Enzo delivers one of the purest and most thrilling driving experiences of any supercar ever built — solidifying its place as a true automotive legend.

Engine
  • 6.0 L Naturally Aspirated V12 (F140B)
  • Power: **651 hp (485 kW)**
  • Torque: 657 Nm
  • Redline: 8,200 rpm
  • Carbon fiber intake & F1-inspired design
Transmission
  • 6-speed "F1" automated manual gearbox
  • Paddle shifters
  • Rear-wheel drive
  • Shift time: 150 milliseconds
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: **3.6 seconds**
  • 0–200 km/h: **9.5 seconds**
  • Top Speed: **350 km/h**
  • Quarter mile: ~11.0 seconds
Chassis & Suspension
  • Carbon-fiber monocoque chassis
  • Push-rod front & rear suspension
  • Active aerodynamic system
Brakes
  • Brembo carbon-ceramic brakes
  • Ventilated discs
  • Track-oriented stopping performance
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,702 mm
  • Width: 2,035 mm
  • Height: 1,147 mm
  • Weight: 1,365 kg
Interior & Features
  • Carbon fiber racing-style interior
  • Lightweight bucket seats
  • Minimalist performance-focused cabin
  • F1 traction control & driving electronics
