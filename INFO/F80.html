Ferrari F80 Concept

The **Ferrari F80 Concept** is a futuristic hypercar vision created by designer Adriano Raeli. Although never officially produced by Ferrari, the F80 showcases what a next-generation Ferrari flagship could look like: extreme aerodynamics, lightweight construction, and hybrid performance beyond the LaFerrari.


The concept proposes a twin-turbo V8 paired with a KERS hybrid system, producing an astonishing **1,200 horsepower**. Its ultra-aggressive bodywork, Formula 1–inspired aero, and sleek proportions represent Ferrari’s potential design direction for a future hypercar.


With race-derived styling, low-slung stance, and futuristic ducting, the Ferrari F80 stands as one of the most radical Ferrari concept designs ever imagined.

Engine
  • 3.0 L Twin-Turbo V8 Hybrid (concept)
  • Combined Output: 1,200 hp
  • 800 hp from combustion engine
  • 400 hp from KERS hybrid system
  • Mid-rear engine layout
Transmission
  • 7-speed dual-clutch transmission (DCT)
  • Active torque vectoring
  • Rear-wheel drive
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: 2.2 seconds
  • Top Speed: ~500 km/h (theoretical)
  • 0–200 km/h: ~5.8 seconds
  • Extreme aerodynamic downforce concept
Chassis & Suspension
  • Carbon-fiber monocoque chassis
  • Active aerodynamics with sliding aero panels
  • Push-rod suspension concept
  • High-efficiency airflow channeling through body
Brakes
  • Carbon-ceramic racing brakes
  • Regenerative hybrid braking (concept)
  • Track-oriented cooling system
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,700 mm (approx.)
  • Width: 2,000 mm
  • Height: 1,000 mm
  • Estimated Weight: 800 kg (concept target)
Interior & Features
  • Minimalist F1-style cockpit
  • Fixed carbon bucket seats
  • HUD-style futuristic driver interface
  • Advanced stability and aero management systems
