Nissan Silvia S15

The Nissan Silvia S15, produced from 1999 to 2002, is the final and most advanced generation of the Silvia lineup. Known worldwide for its lightweight chassis, balanced handling, and tunability, the S15 became an icon in drifting and Japanese car culture.


Featuring the legendary SR20DET turbocharged 2.0L engine, the S15 delivered sharp throttle response, strong torque, and impressive upgrade potential. Its rear-wheel-drive layout and finely tuned multi-link suspension made it one of the best-handling sports cars of its era.


Today, the S15 is highly sought after due to its rarity, performance potential, and iconic status in motorsport, especially drifting. Its combination of style, capability, and tuning freedom has cemented it as one of Nissan’s most beloved sports cars.

Engine
  • 2.0 L SR20DET Turbocharged Inline-4
  • Power: 250 hp (Spec-R)
  • Torque: 275 Nm
  • High tuning potential (400+ hp capable)
Transmission
  • 6-speed manual (Spec-R)
  • 5-speed manual (Spec-S)
  • 4-speed automatic optional
  • Rear-wheel drive
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: ~5.5–6.0 seconds
  • Top Speed: ~240 km/h
  • Quarter Mile: ~14.2 seconds (stock)
Chassis & Suspension
  • Front: MacPherson strut
  • Rear: Multi-link independent suspension
  • Excellent platform for drifting
Brakes
  • Ventilated disc brakes (front & rear)
  • ABS available
  • Aftermarket big brake kits widely used
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,515 mm
  • Width: 1,690 mm
  • Height: 1,280 mm
  • Wheelbase: 2,525 mm
  • Weight: 1,200–1,270 kg
Interior & Features
  • Driver-focused cockpit
  • Lightweight sports seats
  • Analog performance gauges
  • Simple, clean interior layout
