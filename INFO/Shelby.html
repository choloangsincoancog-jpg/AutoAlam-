1967 Shelby GT500

The 1967 Shelby GT500 is one of the most legendary American muscle cars ever built. Developed by Carroll Shelby, it represented the high-performance evolution of the Ford Mustang, blending aggressive styling, racing heritage, and big-block power.

Under the hood sits the powerful 428 cubic-inch “Police Interceptor” V8 equipped with dual Holley four-barrel carburetors. This setup delivered massive torque and exhilarating straight-line performance, making the GT500 one of the fastest American cars of the 1960s.

The 1967 model year was the first GT500 ever produced, making it extremely iconic and highly desirable. With fewer than 2,050 units built, it remains one of the rarest and most collectible Shelby Mustangs today.

Engine
  • 428 cu in (7.0 L) V8 "Police Interceptor"
  • Dual Holley 4-barrel carburetors
  • Power: ~355 hp (factory rated)
  • Torque: ~569 Nm
Transmission
  • 4-speed manual
  • 3-speed automatic (optional)
  • Rear-wheel drive
Performance
  • 0–100 km/h: ~6.5 seconds
  • Top Speed: ~215 km/h
  • Quarter Mile: ~14.7 seconds
Chassis
  • Modified Mustang unibody
  • Front: Independent suspension with coil springs
  • Rear: Leaf springs
Brakes
  • Front: Power-assisted disc brakes
  • Rear: Drum brakes
Dimensions
  • Length: 4,722 mm
  • Width: 1,829 mm
  • Height: 1,346 mm
  • Wheelbase: 2,743 mm
  • Weight: ~1,540 kg
